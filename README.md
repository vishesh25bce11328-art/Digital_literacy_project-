## Project Overview

This project was completed as part of my Digital Literacy course. The main aim of this project
was to understand and use different digital tools that are useful for students in real life.

While doing this project, I got hands-on experience with platforms like GitHub, Canva, Google 
Forms, and coding websites. It helped me improve both my technical skills and my understanding
of how digital tools are used in academics and professional work.

---


## Tasks Completed

### Task 1: Infographic Creation

In this task, I created an infographic using Canva on the topic of digital literacy.
Through this, I learned how to present information in a visual and simple way. It also helped me understand the importance of design and clarity while sharing information.


### Task 2: Profile Creation

I created my profiles on GitHub, Kaggle, and LinkedIn.
This task helped me understand how to build an online presence and how these platforms are used by students and professionals. I also uploaded screenshots of all profiles as proof.


### Task 3: Coding & Google Form

For coding, I solved a beginner-level problem on HackerRank. This improved my logical thinking and basic programming understanding.

I also created a Google Form on digital literacy awareness with 5 questions. The form included both multiple-choice and short-answer questions. After sharing the form, I checked the responses in Google Sheets.


### Task 4: Email Etiquette & Social Media

In this task, I wrote two professional emails:

* One for requesting an assignment extension
* One for applying for an internship

This helped me understand how to write formal emails properly.

I also created a checklist of Do’s and Don’ts for social media usage, which is important for students to maintain a good digital image.


### Task 5: Cybercrime Awareness

In this task, I worked on a cybercrime topic and wrote a case study explaining how the crime happens and its effects.

I also created a prevention checklist with safety tips, especially for online payments and general internet usage. I learned about reporting cybercrime through official portals and helplines.
